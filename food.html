Chicharon

Extremely popular in Spain and Latin America, chicharon are fried pork rinds. They can also be made from chicken, beef, or mutton. Filipinos love to eat this crunchy snack with beer.

Chicken Adobo

One of the most well-known dishes on our list, chicken adobo's distinctive flavors comes from a slow cooking process-plus plenty of vinegar, crushed garlic, soy sauce, and black pepper.

Pancit

Pancit, or pansit, are noodles that can be mixed with whatever meat or vegetable your stomach desires. The name originated from the Hookien word 'pian e sit', meaning 'something conveniently cooked'.

Lumpia

Lumpia is a delicious deep-fried spring roll, stuffed with a mixture of minced meat and chopped vegetables.

Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew with vegetables, oxtail, and a thick savory peanut sauce. Originating before the Spanish arrived to the Philippines, kare-kare is a Filipino comfort food.

Sisig

Sisig is a spicy, fatty meat dish with pig head and liver marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, calamansi juice, and chili peppers. Also known as the 'golden lime,' calamansi is a citrus fruit that's commonly used in Filipino lemonade.

Sinigang

Often associated with tamarind, sinigang is a sour and savory Filipino stew made with onions, tomatoes, eggplant, spinach, ginger, and fish sauce.

Arroz Caldo

Similar to Vietnamese congee, arroz caldo is a chicken and rice porridge stewed in broth, ginger, chives, and occassionally saffron. The name 'arroz caldo' literally translates to 'rice soup' in English.

Lechon

National Dish of the Philippines. Lechon is a famous pork dish that begins with a suckling pig that is roasted over charcoal.

Balut

Balut is a developing embryo that is boiled and eaten from the shell. It originated from and is commonly sold as street food in the Philippines. Often served with beer.

Kwek-Kwek

Kwek-Kwek is a beloved Filipino street food made of hard-boiled eggs covered in a savory orange batter that is then deep fried. This is usually dipped in vinegar and a special sauce.

Fishball

White fishballs are larger in size and made with white fish such as Spanish mackerel, pounded many times until the fish is a fine paste. The balls are then cooked by boiling.

Tempura

Tempura is a Japanese dish usually consisting of seafood or vegetables that have been battered and deep fried.

Barbeque

Barbecue or Barbeque is a cooking method, a style of food, and a name for a meal or gathering at which this style of food is cooked and served.

Steamed Corn

Steaming corn on the cob is one of the easiest and most delicious ways to cook corn. It is one of the Philippine's Finest.

Mani

Peanuts are strangely satiating when eaten warm, the tongue puckering from the excess of salt. The peanuts can be boiled and sold still in their shells, or fried with lots of garlic chips.

Isaw

Isaw is a street food from the Philippines, made from barbecued pig or chicken intestines. The intestines are cleaned, turned inside out, and cleaned again, repeating the process several times; they are then either boiled, then grilled,or immediately grilled om sticks.

Dirty Ice Cream

Dirty Ice Cream also known as the Sorbetes is the traditional variation of ice cream made in the Philippines.

Taho

Taho is a Philippine snack food made of fresh soft/silken tofu, arnibal and sago pearl. This staple comfort food is a signature sweet and taho peddlers can be found all over the country.

Banana Cue

Banana cue is made with deep fried bananas coated in caramelized brown sugar. The bananas used for this recipe are Saba bananas, which are very commonly used for cooking in the Philippines. It is usually skewered on a bamboo stick, and sold on the strets.
