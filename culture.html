Ragsaksakan

Ragsaksakan means "Merriment". This dance portrays the walk of the industrious Kalingga women, carrying water pots on their heads and wearing the colorful hand-woven "blankets of life" around their necks. This dance would be performed after a successful headhunts.

Mandadawak

Although no longer practiced, headhunting is a long tradition among the mountain tribes of Northern Philippines and associated with it are various war dances. The Kalingas, another mountain tribe of Northern Philippines has a ritual dance called idaw.

Tinikling

Tinikling is a traditional Philippine Folkdance which involves two people beating, tapping and sliding bamboo poles on the ground and against each other in coordinations with one or more dancers who step over and in between the poles in a dance.

Maglalatik

An indigenous dance from Philippines which a coconut shell halves are secured onto the dancers hands and on vests upon which are hung four or six more coconut shells halves.

Pandanggo sa ilaw

This dance was translated as the dance of lights which is a waltz style, playful folkdance that showcased a unique fusion of local and western indigenous dance forms.

Ati-Atihan Festival

It is the oldest festival in the Philippines. Ati-Atihan stirred the creations of many other festivals in the Philippines. Among them are the Sinulog in Cebu and the Dinagyang in Iloilo.

Panagbenga Festival

The term Pangabenga is of Kankanaey origin, meaning "season of blooming". The festival, held in February, was created as a tribute to the city's flowers and as a way to rise up from devastation of the 1990 Luzon earthquake.

Sinulog Festival

It is essentially a dance ritual which remembers the Filipino people's pagan past and their acceptance of Christianity.

Dinagyang Festival

Dinagyang is celebrated to honor the Christianization of the natives and to respect the Holy Child Jesus. It is an annual event when the whole town rejoices, shouting their pride of being an Ilonggo and telling their culture.

Pintados Festival

Dancers paint their bodies in patterns that resemble armor to present past warriors. The festival recalls Pre-Spanish history with the native Leytenos from wars, epics and folk religions.

Bayanihan

The Bayanihan is a Filipino costum derived from a Filipino word "bayan", which means nation, town or community. The term bayanihan itself literally means "being in a bayan", which refers to the spirit of communal unity, work and cooperation.

Mano

Mano or pagmamano is a "honoring-gesture used in Filipino culture performed as a sign of respect to elders and as a way of requesting blessing from the elder.

Harana

The Harana is serenade tradition in rural areas of the Philippines in which young men may formally meet single lady visitors.

Marriage

The legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners in a personal relationship.

Sungka

Sungka is a popular game in the Philippines. It involves dropping small stones or cowrie shells into large holes on a ling canoe-shaped board.

Patintero

Patintero, also known as harangang-taga or tubigan, is a traditional Filipino children's game.

Luksong Baka

Translated as "jumping over a cow" is a traditional Pinoy game that involves players jumping over one player called "baka" or cow.

Sipa

Sipa pertains to the traditional Pinoy game and as translated as the game of kick.

Piko

Piko is a game where the players draw a big image on the floor filled mostly of box-like shapes, which they will hop on and around using a single foot.

Tumbang Preso

Tumbang preso is a traditional Pinoy game that we used to play most of the time on the streets with very little vehicular traffic.
