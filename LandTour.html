Barasaoin Church

The Barasaoin Church in Malolos, Bulacan is considered the most imortant religios buildings in the Philippines because of its role during the important events that occured in the country.

Malacanang Palace

The Malacanang Palace or Palasyo ng Malakanyang is the official residence of the President of the Philippines.

MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park

Also called as Leyte Landing Memorial Park and MacArthur Park, is a protected area that commemorates the landing of the allied forces under the command of Gen. Douglas MacArthur.

Taal Lake and Taal Volcano

Taal Lake in Batangas is one of the most famous tourist attractions in the Philippines. The beautiful landscape of the lake which sorrounds Taal Volcano can also be seen in an overlooking view from Tagaytay City in Cavite.

Luneta and Intramuros

Luneta and Intramuros are two historically significant places, not only in Manila, but in the entire Philippines.

Aguinaldo Shrine

The shrine is the ancestral home of General Emilio Aguinaldo, the first officially recognized president in the Philippines.

Vigan Heritage Village

Those who have taken up Philippine History will undoubtedly recognize these row Spanish era houses that have been preserved for future generations of Filipinos and also the world.

Benigno Aquino Monument

The monument of Benigno Aquino in Makati City commemorates the great sacrifice of Philippine senator Benigno "Ninoy"Aquino to fight for and earn democracy for the Filipino people during the Marcos regime.

Sandugo Shrine

Sandugo referred to the blood compact that took place between the Spanish explorer Miguel Lopez de Legazpi and the chieftain of Bohol. It was considered to be the first ever treaty of friendship between the Filipinos and the Spaniards.

Mactan Shrine

The Mactan Shrine or Lapu-Lapu Shrine. Lapu-Lapu, a native leader who defeated the Spanish soldiers and killed the Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan back in 1521. The event is now famously referred to as the Battle of Mactan.

Jose Rizal Shrine

The Jose Rizal Memorial Protected Landscape in Dapitan, Zamboanga del Norte, is the farm site where the country's national hero was exiled for four years after being accused by the Spanish authorities for plotting the Philippine Revolution in Manila.

Sultan Kudarat Monument

Sultan Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat,the sultan was known as a Filipino hero who fought against the Spanish invaders and defended his Islamic faith. He was also considered one of the greatest leaders in Mindanao as he was able to unite the Muslims.

Corregidor

Known to be the "the Rock", Corregidor is known for its important historical attractions. When the Japanese invaded the Philippines, Corregidor became the headquarters of the Allied forces and also the seat of Philippine Commonwealth government.

Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago, located in Intramuros, is a famous tourist destination in the Philippines. It is a historical structure that is part of the city's famous wall.

San Pedro Cathedral

The San Pedro Cathedral is the oldest church in Mindanao. The cathedral was declared by the Philippine government as one of the country's national cultural treasre.

Fuerza de San Pedro

Fuerza de San Pedro is a military defense structure in Cebu, built by the Spanish under the command of Miguel Lopez de Legazpi, first governor of the Captaincy General of the Philippines.

The Heritage of Cebu

The monument is located in Plaza Parain at Colon St.,Cebu City. It showcases the significant and symbolic events in the history of Cebu from the time of Rajah Humabon to the recent beatification of Cebuano martyr Pedro Calunsod.

The Cathedral Museum of Cebu

The Cathedral Museum of Cebu is a museum in Downtown Cebu City in the Philippines, (re)opened in November 2006. It is the ecclesiastical museum of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Cebu.

Baguio and Lion's Head in Kennon Road

It is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Baguio, located at the outskirts of the city, it is also a popular stopover for quick photo souverniers before entering the BAguio's downtown area.

Fort Asturias

The Fort of the Princess of Asturias is one of the remarkable vestiges of the Spanish occupation of Jolo. Built in honor of the Princess of Spain, this building was converted into a U.S. military headquarters, where the Spanish forces left the island in 1899.
